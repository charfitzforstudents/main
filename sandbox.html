Meet Char

Grandmother. Educator.
Finance Geek. Business Leader. Professor.

My Values were shaped by my hero – my dad who was part of the Greatest Generation.

I object to a hidden agendas that plague our public-school systems today. Let me be clear, there is a set of values that undergird my priorities – it's the values and perspective of my father who was a Boy Scout and who enlisted in the U.S. Navy when he was 17 to serve in WWII. It's the values that undergird our constitution…the values of democracy, equal protection under the law and freedom of speech. I believe public education is the cornerstone of democracy and I plan to stand up for it for the good of our children and the future.

That's why shared governance, community engagement, transparency and financial oversight matter as the key responsibilities of the school board.

Char Fitzwater, Ph.D., Uniquely Qualified

Char is uniquely qualified as an educator, business leader and financial professional to serve as a Trustee for the Charleston County School District (CCSD). She believes in the importance of strong public education for all our children. Char, her husband and their two children have benefited from their public educations. She has two grandchildren who attend CCSD public schools, and one who attends public school and two who will when they become school-age. Most importantly, Char prioritizes transparent, capable, collaborative governance that intentionally engages with the community and supports strengthening our district.

Education & Leadership

Char graduated cum laude with a bachelor's degree in education from Vanderbilt University. After teaching briefly, she pursued an MBA at The University of Kansas. She had a successful 22-year business career including finance and strategy leadership roles at a Fortune 150 company and serving as Chief Financial Officer/Chief Information Officer of a regional insurance company. Concurrent with pursuing her PhD in Organization and Management, Char stepped into teaching business, strategy and finance. She taught for 20 years at Immaculata University, where she served as department chair and successfully pursued accreditation for the business programs. Char retired as professor emerita.

Financial & Strategy Acumen

Char worked collaboratively with multidisciplinary teams across organizations to achieve success in complex situations. Char was an executive in a property-casualty insurance company that elevated its operations through rigorous financial management, restructuring and leveraging emerging technology. Char led successful pursuit of accreditation of Immaculata University's business programs. She also coached student teams whose entrepreneurial projects included launching a nonprofit serving abused women and their children in Bolivia.

Vision for Educational Excellence

Under the district's leadership, Charleston County School District (CCSD) has made recent progress. However, the data also indicates there is still a long way to go to achieve its own objectives. While we celebrate that CCSD schools rank third in the state, the state ranks 41st in K-12 education nationally. Char is confident we can improve learning experiences for all our children by supporting proactive efforts of the district and developing wider community engagement.

Let's put Char's skills and heart to work for the betterment of Charleston County public schools.

Notes for the Team

What changed & why

Paragraphs → Bullets in credential cards

Each credential card had 4–6 sentences. Replacing with tight bullet points cuts reading time by ~60% while keeping every key fact. Visitors can scan in seconds instead of reading for a minute.

"Uniquely Qualified" paragraph condensed

The original paragraph is 5 sentences — several of which repeat what the credential cards already show. The proposed version trims it to 2 sentences that hit the two things readers most need to feel: she's qualified, and she has personal skin in the game. Her values section stays 100% verbatim.

Stat callouts added to photo column

Three quick numbers (40+ years, 3 grandchildren in school, 3 degrees) are the most persuasive shorthand for who Char is. They load instantly for readers who won't read any copy at all.

What to decide as a team

Does the condensed "Uniquely Qualified" paragraph (now 2 sentences) still feel right, or does it lose something important? Is the grandchildren stat phrased correctly (3 in public schools, 2 in CCSD)? And is there any bullet in the credential cards that feels off or missing something Char would want there?
